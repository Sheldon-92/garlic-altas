The Non-Human Protagonist: Butterfat & Solubility

Here, the cow (via butter) provides the medium. The key mechanism is fat solubility.

The Chemical Process: Garlic's organosulfur compounds are highly soluble in fat. When the butter melts, it captures these volatile compounds, preventing them from evaporating and delivering them efficiently to the palate. The fat coats the tongue, extending the flavor experience, while the milk solids brown slightly, adding a nutty depth.
