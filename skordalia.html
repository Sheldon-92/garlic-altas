The Multispecies Dance

The Non-Human Protagonist: Starch Granules & Gelatinization

Unlike Toum which is pure garlic emulsion, Skordalia relies on a bulking agent (potato or stale bread). The starch acts as a physical scaffold.

The Chemical Process: When potatoes are boiled, their starch granules swell and burst (gelatinization). When pounded with garlic and oil, this starch network traps the oil droplets and water, creating a thick, cohesive paste. The garlic remains raw, ensuring maximal enzyme activity and pungency.

Cultural Context

Skordalia is a traditional fasting food, often served with Bakaliaros (fried salt cod) on March 25th (The Annunciation). It provides a sense of richness and satiety during Lent when animal fats are forbidden.
