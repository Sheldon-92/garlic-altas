The Multispecies Dance

The Non-Human Protagonist: Chlorophyll & Acetic Acid

This sauce is a collaboration between the pungent defense of the garlic and the photosynthetic pigment of fresh herbs (cilantro or parsley).

The Chemical Process: The vinegar (acetic acid) plays a dual role. It flavors the sauce, but more importantly, it creates an acidic environment that preserves the vibrant green chlorophyll. However, it's a race against time‚Äîeventually, the acid will degrade the green into a dull olive color, so freshness is key.

Geographic Bridge: Originating in the Canary Islands, this sauce represents the biological exchange between Europe, Africa (cumin), and the Americas (cilantro/peppers).

Cultural Context

Mojo is the definitive condiment of the Canaries, famously paired with Papas Arrugadas (wrinkly potatoes boiled in sea water). The pairing of the salty, starch-heavy potato with the acidic, garlic-rich mojo is a classic study in flavor balance.
